About me

I am an evolutionary biologist interested in how the traits of organisms affect their evolution. In my PhD research, I have investigated how various kinds of noise in molecular traits affect their evolution using custom-made computational models. These projects considered evolution from a population genetic perspective and over relatively short time spans. I have since pivoted to longer time spans. Currently, I am engaged on a data analysis project trying to identify the traits that made ammonites vulnerable to extinction at the end-Cretaceous, but allowed the nautilids to survive.
